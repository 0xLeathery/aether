How It Works

Three steps to your first sovereign connection.

01

Generate Your Identity

Aether creates an Ed25519 keypair stored in your system keychain. No email. No password. No account. Your cryptographic identity lives only on your machine.

02

Share a Secret Code

Paste a one-time invite code to your contact — over Signal, in person, anywhere you trust. The code is how peers find each other without a directory server.

03

Connect Peer-to-Peer

WebRTC negotiates a direct connection between your nodes. Once connected, voice and text travel straight between you — no relay, no middleman, no egress bill.

Built for Sovereignty

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

Voice Chat

Crystal-clear peer-to-peer voice sessions powered by the Opus codec. Sub-50ms latency with no relay servers. Supports up to 8 participants in a P2P mesh.

Text Chat

CRDT-synced messages that survive disconnections. Your conversations are stored locally and sync when peers reconnect — no server to go down, no timeline to scroll past.

Channels

Named channels organize conversations within a swarm. CRDT metadata ensures channel state is consistent across all peers without a central coordinator.

Moderation

Hide or block any peer — locally, instantly, without reporting to anyone. You control your experience. Your decisions stay on your machine.

Contacts

Assign private petnames to your peers. Your contacts use your names for them, not whatever they chose to call themselves. Identity on your terms.

Trade-offs We Chose

Radical honesty about our architectural decisions. These are constraints we chose deliberately.

Simultaneous Presence Required

In practice:

Both peers must be online at the same time to exchange messages. There is no inbox, no store-and-forward, no offline delivery.

Why we chose it:

Pure peer-to-peer sync eliminates relay servers entirely. No egress costs, no central point of failure, no company holding your messages in transit.

Transport-Only Encryption

In practice:

Messages are encrypted in transit between peers. Data at rest on your disk is not encrypted by Aether — your OS and disk encryption handle that.

Why we chose it:

Simplifies the v1 architecture and preserves local search and indexing performance. Full at-rest encryption is a planned future improvement.

Hardware-Bound Identity

In practice:

Your identity keypair lives in your system keychain and cannot be exported or transferred. Losing your device means starting fresh with a new identity.

Why we chose it:

System keychain security (Secure Enclave on macOS, TPM on Windows) makes private keys non-extractable. This is a stronger security guarantee than seed phrases.

8-Peer Voice Limit

In practice:

Voice sessions support a maximum of 8 simultaneous participants. Beyond 8, connection quality degrades due to mesh topology bandwidth requirements.

Why we chose it:

WebRTC P2P mesh requires each peer to maintain N-1 direct connections. 8 is the practical limit before latency and bandwidth costs outpace the benefit of avoiding a server.
